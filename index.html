FAQs

Questions parents usually ask.

If you’re comparing centres, this is the part that matters: what actually happens week to week, and how we handle different ability levels.

How do you decide Foundation vs Higher?

We use a short diagnostic and in-lesson questioning to see where marks are being lost. Some students are working at Higher but have Foundation gaps — we fix the basics fast, then move up.

My child is far behind. Can you still help?

Yes — but we don’t pretend it’s magic. We prioritise the highest-impact topics first (the ones that unlock the most marks), build confidence through structured practice, and track progress so time isn’t wasted.

Do you set homework and revision tasks?

Yes. Homework matches what was taught in the lesson so students don’t practise the wrong method. We focus on exam-style questions rather than endless worksheets.

What happens in a trial session?

We assess current level, teach a short topic to show our approach, then explain the best next steps (1-to-1, small class, or Study Buddy). If we’re not the right fit, we’ll say so.

How do small classes work — will my child get attention?

We keep groups focused and structured. Teaching is clear and short, then students practise while the tutor checks method and fixes mistakes. If a student needs 1-to-1 support, we’ll recommend it.

Which exam board do you teach?

We teach GCSE Maths in an exam-board-aware way. The core maths is the same, and we use exam-style questions aligned to common board formats (including Edexcel-style, AQA-style, and OCR-style).
